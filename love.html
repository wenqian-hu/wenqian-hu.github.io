Project Name // Send Love

Toolkit // Arduino Yún, API

Instructor // Matthew Belanger

Location // Interactive Media Arts, NYU, Shanghai

Course // Developing Web

Time // 1 week, 2017


Project Description

Send Love is an Internet of Things project designed for long distance relationship lovers. Transferring emotions into tangible and physical product, this projects seeks to an alternative way of distant communication other than on-screen.

Ideation

When pressing the “send some love” button from the app, the heart installed in the physical box will light up. Accordingly, when pressing the physical button as a response, the application will receive a notification as a reminder of love.

It also includes weather API and can visualize the weather condition in the city of your significant other.

How to make long-distance communication tangible?

Have you ever found text messages and emojis are not enough to convey your miss and love for another person you care about? Have you ever imagined that you can actually touch the feelings sent from afar? Though technology has advanced our virtual communications to live spontaneously, we sometimes desire for a more emotional and tangible way of showing and receiving love from each other.

With Send Love, you can express your thought to your significant other with one tap. Your love will light up the heart in the physical box. This action is simple but delightful. It brings significant joys to the others, because light is unexpected, it is remotely controlled by someone else, and s/he can gives a response on an analog device.

Send Love can be adopted into different scenarios benefits from its small sizes and simple functions. It can be brought along with you and put on your desk at work to your bedside table. With Send Love, you can give a quick but lovely message without interrupting the work you are doing.
